HISTORY ABOUT RABIES

People have been terrified after being bitten by rabid dogs for ages. However, one must travel far back in time to know that until the 19th century, neither humans nor animals could be accurately diagnosed with the disease, the infectious agent could not be isolated, there was no animal management, and there was no human treatment. Celsus understood that wild animals have a part in the spread of rabies. Wolves have frequently been mentioned in the histories of violent breakouts in wild animals and the ensuing human cases. The majority of developed nations have virtually completely eliminated canine rabies, however there have been more occurrences of wildlife rabies reported. The prevention of wildlife rabies is a different issue because there is no way to vaccinate the tens of thousands of animals required to do so. Through the use of a variety of attenuated and recombinant rabies vaccines, as well as in raccoons and coyotes, the oral vaccination method has eliminated rabies in many Western European countries and eastern Canada. In the underdeveloped world, rabid canines are virtually invariably the source of human rabies cases, although this is no longer the case in the industrialized world.
